Shish Tawook

Description

Shish tawooks are tasty grilled chicken skewers that are served in every restaurant in the Middle East. You can easily make them at home and they'll taste just as good, if not better! Don't be tempted to substitute sour cream for the yogurt, as the yogurt works as a meat tenderizer.

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup plain yogurt
  • ¼ cup lemon juice
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons tomato paste
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt
  • teaspoon dried oregano
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon ground allspice
  • ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 2 pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 2 onions, cut into large chunks
  • large green bell pepper, cut into large chunks
  • cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley

Steps:

  1. Mix yogurt, lemon juice, oil, garlic, tomato paste, salt, oregano, pepper, allspice, cinnamon, and cardamom in a large bowl. Add chicken and toss to coat; transfer into a resealable plastic bag, squeeze out excess air, and seal the bag. Marinate in the refrigerator for 4 hours.
  2. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  3. Thread chicken, onions, and bell pepper onto metal skewers.
  4. Cook on preheated grill until chicken is golden and no longer pink in the center, about 5 minutes on each side. Sprinkle parsley over the skewers.
